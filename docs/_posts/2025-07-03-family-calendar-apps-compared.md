You've tried them all. Google Calendar with color-coded family members. Cozi with shared lists. FamilyWall with its photo sharing. Maybe even a shared Apple Calendar.

They all have the same fatal flaw: **you still have to manually enter everything.**

## The real problem isn't the calendar

The problem isn't where you store your family events. It's how you get information INTO the system in the first place.

Think about how family logistics actually arrive:
- A WhatsApp message from another parent: "Training moved to 5pm Thursday"
- A school email with 6 different dates and deadlines buried in 3 paragraphs
- A screenshot of a schedule from a sports club app
- A quick thought while driving: "need to book dentist for next week"
- A forwarded email from your partner: "can you handle this?"

None of the popular family calendar apps handle this input problem. They all assume you'll sit down and type "Football training, Thursday, 5pm, Sports Hall" into a form.

## What parents actually need

A family organizer that:

1. **Accepts messy input** — share from any app, forward emails, speak naturally
2. **Extracts the meaning** — doesn't just store text, but understands dates, people, actions
3. **Lets you verify quickly** — shows what it understood, you approve or fix
4. **Handles changes** — when plans change (and they always do), catches the update
5. **Shows what matters today** — not a grid of colored blocks, but "here's what needs attention"

Nabbo isn't trying to replace Google Calendar. It's the **input layer** that feeds your family plan. The messy information comes in → Nabbo structures it → you approve → it's in your plan.

## The mental load is real

Studies show that in most families, one parent carries the "mental load" — the invisible work of remembering, tracking, and coordinating everything. School dates, activity schedules, payment deadlines, what to pack, who's picking up whom.

This isn't a calendar problem. It's an **information processing** problem. And that's exactly what AI is good at.
