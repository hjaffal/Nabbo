The school sent the email two weeks ago. The deadline was yesterday. You just found it at the bottom of your inbox.

Sound familiar? You're not alone. A survey of UK parents found that 67% have missed at least one school deadline in the past year. Permission slips, payment deadlines, costume days, non-uniform days, trip forms — they pile up.

Here are 5 strategies that actually work.

## 1. Create a dedicated school email folder

Don't let school emails mix with everything else. Set up a filter:
- Gmail: Filter by sender → label "School" → star it
- Outlook: Rule by sender → move to "School" folder

Then check this folder every Sunday evening. Nothing else lives there — just school communications.

## 2. Process immediately, don't "read later"

The moment you see a school email, do ONE thing with it:
- Add the date to your calendar, OR
- Forward it to your family app, OR
- Reply "done" to yourself as a reminder

"I'll deal with it later" is where deadlines go to die.

## 3. Use a shared family calendar with alerts

Put every school date on a shared calendar that both parents can see. Set alerts:
- 1 week before (for things that need preparation)
- 1 day before (final reminder)
- Morning of (for things to bring)

## 4. Forward school emails to an AI assistant

This is the modern approach. Apps like Nabbo give you a dedicated email address. Forward the school email → AI extracts every date, deadline, and requirement → you approve in seconds.

One email might contain:
- 📅 School trip next Friday
- 💷 £5 due Wednesday
- 📝 Permission slip by Thursday
- 🎒 Pack lunch needed

All extracted automatically, each with its own reminder.

## 5. Do a weekly "family admin" session

Every Sunday, spend 10 minutes:
1. Check this week's events
2. Check what needs preparing
3. Check who's handling what
4. Check if anything has changed

This one habit prevents 90% of missed deadlines.

## The real solution: reduce the manual work

The common thread in all these strategies is reducing the gap between "information received" and "action planned." The longer that gap, the more likely you'll forget.

AI tools close that gap to nearly zero. Information arrives → AI processes it → you approve → reminders set. No manual entry, no "I'll do it later," no forgotten deadlines.

## What matters most

Perfect systems don't exist. You'll still occasionally miss things. But going from "miss something every week" to "miss something once a term" is a massive quality-of-life improvement for the whole family.

Pick one strategy from this list and try it this week. Your future self will thank you.
